Securing Employment

Having a felony on their record often bars individuals from stable jobs after release.

Benefits of Employment

Having stable employment not only allows an ex-felon to maintain a more comfortable life outside of prison, but it also encourages them to break the cycle and not reoffend. Despite the benefits of creating more job opportunities for this demographic, both for the offender and for society, being formerly incarcerated makes an employer far less likely to hire an applicant, even if they advertise that they provide equal opportunities for all

Breaking the Cycle

To assist with combating this type of discrimination, many cities in the U.S have put in place “Ban the Box” laws. These laws make it illegal for employers to request applicants to disclose their criminal history on the job application form. However, the specifics of this law vary by locality. While some states ban doing a background check on an individual until after a job is offered, other states only require that this check is not done before their first interview and can only inquire about convictions within a certain time frame.
